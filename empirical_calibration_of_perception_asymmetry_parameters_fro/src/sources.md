# Sources

## 1. Measuring the Measures: A Meta-Analytic Investigation of the Measures of Outgroup Homogeneity (Boldry, Gaertner, Quinn, 2007)

**URL:** https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/1368430207075153

**Summary:** Comprehensive meta-analysis of 177 effect sizes from 173 independent samples and 12,078 participants showing that perceived dispersion measures yield d ≈ 0.45 while range measures yield d ≈ 0.20, documenting critical variation by measurement method.

---

## 2. Outgroup Homogeneity - Wikipedia overview with meta-analytic findings

**URL:** https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Out-group_homogeneity

**Summary:** Synthesis of meta-analytic findings showing overall effect size r ≈ 0.10 but substantially larger effects (d > 0.40) in natural-group contexts compared to minimal laboratory groups.

---

## 3. American partisans vastly underestimate the diversity of other partisans' policy attitudes (Political Science Research and Methods)

**URL:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/political-science-research-and-methods/article/american-partisans-vastly-underestimate-the-diversity-of-other-partisans-policy-attitudes/CD71B9D75D10CFA0A9A07501E1CCD81A

**Summary:** Quantitative documentation that American partisans vastly underestimate within-party diversity without consistently overestimating mean extremity, establishing the diversity perception asymmetry as distinct from mean position bias.

---

## 4. The Perception Gap - More in Common (2019)

**URL:** https://perceptiongap.us/

**Summary:** Large-scale representative survey data showing specific perception gaps (e.g., 93% actual vs. 35% estimated Republican agreement on values), with documentation of 20% Fox News effect, media consumption correlations, and political engagement moderation.

---

## 5. Echo chambers, filter bubbles, and polarisation: a literature review (Reuters Institute)

**URL:** https://reutersinstitute.politics.ox.ac.uk/echo-chambers-filter-bubbles-and-polarisation-literature-review

**Summary:** Systematic review documenting conflicting evidence on whether algorithms reduce exposure diversity, with evidence that radicalized communities seeking like-minded others show strongest echo chamber effects.

---

## 6. Do social media undermine social cohesion? A critical review

**URL:** https://spssi.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/sipr.12091

**Summary:** Finding that in homogenous offline communities, disconnecting from diverse online networks increases intergroup animosity, suggesting online diversity buffers perception asymmetries.

---

## 7. Online Intergroup Polarization Across Political Fault Lines: An Integrative Review

**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8559783/

**Summary:** Synthesis documenting that online technologies amplify intragroup validation while increasing intergroup conflict salience through exposure to outgroup views.

---

## 8. 'Seen one, seen 'em all': political outgroup homogeneity and negative affect (2025)

**URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/01402382.2025.2489869

**Summary:** Recent cross-domain documentation of outgroup homogeneity effects in political contexts with examination of domain variations including racial, ethnic, and gender groups.

---

## 9. Minimal Group Paradigm - iResearchNet overview

**URL:** https://psychology.iresearchnet.com/social-psychology/group/minimal-group-paradigm/

**Summary:** Description of minimal group paradigm findings showing that majority members perceive outgroups as less variable while minority members show opposite pattern, documenting status moderation.

---

## 10. Development and Validation of the Affective Polarization Scale

**URL:** https://rips-irsp.com/articles/10.5334/irsp.926

**Summary:** Validated 15-item, 7-point Likert scale with three dimensions (social distance, aversion, incivility) yielding Cronbach's α = 0.96 and McDonald's ωt = 0.97 across all subscales.

---

## 11. Accuracy in the judgment of in-group and out-group variability

**URL:** https://psycnet.apa.org/doiLanding?doi=10.1037/0022-3514.61.3.366

**Summary:** Park and Rothbart percentage estimation methodology for measuring perceived group variability through stereotypic vs. counterstereotypic attitude agreement estimates.

---

## 12. A New Measure of Affective Polarization (American Political Science Review)

**URL:** https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-political-science-review/article/new-measure-of-affective-polarization/DEF7FCC26D4F09BDE5603BCC02B4765D

**Summary:** Documentation of three primary measurement approaches: feeling thermometers (0-100 scales), trait ratings across dimensions, and social distance items measuring behavioral comfort with outgroup members.

---

## 13. Motivated Skepticism in the Evaluation of Political Beliefs (Taber & Lodge, 2006)

**URL:** https://fbaum.unc.edu/teaching/articles/AJPS-2006-Taber.pdf

**Summary:** Foundational experimental evidence that confirmation and disconfirmation biases in political reasoning strengthen with political sophistication, showing attitude polarization especially among passionate and knowledgeable individuals.

---

## 14. Understanding and combating misperceived polarization

**URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7935088/

**Summary:** Synthesis distinguishing false polarization from actual polarization, showing that perceived variance/diversity underestimation is primary driver distinct from mean position overestimation.

---

## 15. Americans experience a false social reality by underestimating popular climate policy support by nearly half (Nature Communications)

**URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-022-32412-y

**Summary:** Large-scale evidence that 80-90% of Americans underestimate public support, with estimates of 37-43% when actual support is 66-80%, quantifying ~50 percentage-point perception gaps on public opinion consensus.

---

## 16. Improving intergroup attitudes and reducing stereotype threat: An integrated contact model

**URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/10463280802547171

**Summary:** Meta-analytic evidence that intergroup contact reduces perceived homogeneity and decreases perception asymmetries through increased ingroup differentiation and outgroup individuation.

---

## 17. Mitigating the Default? The Influence of Ingroup Diversity on Outgroup Trust

**URL:** https://rips-irsp.com/articles/10.5334/irsp.520

**Summary:** Evidence that cognitive humility, measured ingroup diversity perception, and individual differences in intellectual openness correlate negatively with outgroup homogeneity effect magnitude.

---
